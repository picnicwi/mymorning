Human Scientists Still Outperform Best AI Agents on Complex Research Tasks

A new study published in Nature shows that human scientists continue to trounce the best AI agents on complex, multi-step research tasks. While AI excels at narrow, well-defined sub-tasks, it struggles with the iterative judgment and creative problem-solving central to scientific discovery. Researchers note the gap is narrowing but remains substantial in open-ended domains.

Banking & Financial Services

GenAI crosses the tipping point in banking — adoption jumps from 8% to 58%, investment surges, and regulators signal a green light.

Banking & FinServ 01 / 03
— Finastra / Fintech Global

58% of Banks Have Fully Implemented GenAI in at Least One Core Function

New industry data shows 58% of banking organizations have fully implemented generative AI in at least one business function as of 2026, compared to 45% in 2023 and just 8% in 2024. Banks are integrating large language models into underwriting automation, risk analytics, fraud detection, KYC processes, and real-time customer assistance via intelligent chat and voice interfaces. Governance and integration standards are now being mandated at the core process level, not just run as pilots.

Why it matters The jump from 8% to 58% tactical adoption in two years signals that GenAI has crossed the tipping point from experimentation to operational deployment across the banking sector.
Banking & FinServ 02 / 03
— FinTech Magazine

82% of Banking Risk Leaders Expect AI Investment to Grow Over 25% in Next Three Years

A survey of banking compliance and risk leaders finds 82% expect their AI investment to grow by more than 25% over the next two to three years, with a further 10% anticipating more modest growth. Banks are linking generative AI directly to core banking platforms and unified data stores to improve accuracy, speed, and consistency of AI outputs. Agentic AI remains at an earlier adoption stage but is firmly on the roadmap.

Why it matters Sustained double-digit AI investment growth in risk and compliance functions will accelerate vendor consolidation and raise the bar for technology partners serving financial institutions.
Banking & FinServ 03 / 03
— FDIC

FDIC Speech Highlights How Regulators Are Keeping Pace with AI-Driven Innovation

An FDIC speech titled 'Innovation at the Speed of Markets' outlines how regulators are evolving frameworks to keep pace with rapid AI and fintech adoption in banking. The FDIC emphasized the need for adaptive supervision approaches as banks deploy AI in credit decisioning, fraud, and customer-facing functions. The regulator signaled openness to innovation while stressing the importance of consumer protection guardrails.

Why it matters Regulatory clarity from the FDIC on AI in banking reduces implementation uncertainty and is a green light for financial institutions that have been waiting on compliance guidance before expanding AI programs.

Data Platforms

Data Platforms 01 / 03
— Databricks Blog

Databricks Lakebase Goes GA at FabCon 2026 — A Postgres Database Built for AI Agents

At FabCon 2026, Azure Databricks announced the general availability of Lakebase — a managed, serverless Postgres service built directly into the lakehouse, designed to serve as the transactional system of record for AI agents. Additional announcements included Genie Code for agentic data engineering, Lakeflow Connect Free Tier, and new integrations with Excel and Microsoft Teams. The platform extends Azure Databricks into Microsoft 365 so governed data and AI insights are available where users already work.

Why it matters Lakebase bridges the gap between analytical lakehouses and transactional databases within a single governed platform — a critical building block for production-grade agentic AI applications.
Data Platforms 02 / 03
— Microsoft Fabric Blog

Microsoft and Databricks Advance OneLake Interoperability with Unity Catalog Integration

Microsoft and Databricks announced that native reading from OneLake through Azure Databricks Unity Catalog is now in public preview. Both companies are making the case that transactional and analytical data should live under the same governance, access model, and platform. The collaboration reflects a broader industry shift toward unified data platforms optimized for AI workloads.

Why it matters OneLake-Unity Catalog interoperability removes a major data silo between Microsoft Fabric and Databricks environments, simplifying governance for enterprises running both platforms.
Data Platforms 03 / 03
— Azure Blog

FabCon 2026: Microsoft Unifies Databases and Fabric on a Single Data Platform

Microsoft used FabCon and SQLCon 2026 to announce a strategic push to unify its databases and Microsoft Fabric under a single integrated data platform. The Register reported Microsoft is positioning Fabric as a 'multi-database wrangling hub,' consolidating formerly separate SQL, analytics, and AI data services. Microsoft also acquired Osmos, an AI-driven data engineering platform, folding its capabilities into Fabric's OneLake architecture.

Why it matters Consolidating SQL, analytics, and AI data services into Fabric reduces integration complexity for enterprise data teams and strengthens Microsoft's competitive position against Snowflake and Databricks.

Microsoft introduced Azure HorizonDB, a new AI-optimized database with built-in vector indexing as part of its April 2026 Azure platform updates.
