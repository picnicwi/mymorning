Banking & FinServ 01 / 03
Finastra / Fintech Global

GenAI Banking Adoption Surges: 78% of Banks Now Deploying Tactically

A striking 78% of banks are now adopting generative AI tactically in 2026, a dramatic leap from only 8% in 2024. Full implementation in at least one banking function has reached 58% of organizations, compared to 45% in 2023. Banks are integrating large language models across underwriting automation, risk analytics, fraud detection, KYC processes, and personalized customer interactions.

Why it matters The explosive acceleration of GenAI adoption in banking over just two years signals that the technology has crossed from experimentation to operational necessity for financial institutions.
Banking & FinServ 02 / 03
Fintech Global

Agentic AI Is Banking's Next Investment Wave, But Adoption Lags GenAI

While generative AI is now widespread in banking, agentic AI—systems that act autonomously on behalf of users—remains nascent, with only 31% of banks having moved forward with implementation. However, investment intent is strong: 82% of banking compliance and risk leaders expect their AI budgets to grow by more than 25% over the next two to three years. Agentic AI is seen as critical for fully automated loan processing, compliance workflows, and real-time treasury management.

Why it matters Banks that move early on agentic AI will gain significant efficiency and speed advantages in back-office operations and compliance, areas with massive cost implications.
Banking & FinServ 03 / 03
McKinsey / PwC

AI ROI in Banking: 78% of Investing Banks Report Positive Returns Within 18 Months

Seven in ten commercial banks have now adopted AI in at least one core banking function, and 78% of those investing in AI report positive ROI within 18 months of deployment. McKinsey estimates generative AI could generate $200–$340 billion in additional annual value for the banking sector alone—equivalent to 9–15% of operating profits. Banks linking GenAI directly to core banking platforms and unified data stores are seeing the strongest outcomes in accuracy, speed, and consistency.

Why it matters Proven ROI data at scale removes the 'proof of concept' barrier for banking AI investment and shifts the conversation toward competitive parity and differentiation.

Data Platforms

Microsoft Fabric reaches 31,000 customers, Databricks launches Lakebase GA, and OneLake interoperability reshapes enterprise data architecture.

Data Platforms 01 / 03
Microsoft Fabric

FabCon 2026: Microsoft Fabric Surpasses 31,000 Customers, Launches Fabric IQ

Microsoft Fabric, just two and a half years after reaching general availability, now serves more than 31,000 customers and remains the fastest-growing data platform in Microsoft history. The FabCon 2026 conference introduced Fabric IQ, a semantic modeling layer that gives AI agents the contextual understanding needed to reason accurately over enterprise data. Microsoft also announced expanded OneLake interoperability, reinforcing the platform's push toward a unified, single-copy data architecture.

Why it matters Fabric IQ transforms Microsoft Fabric from a data integration platform into an AI-reasoning foundation, positioning it as the connective tissue for enterprise AI agents.
Data Platforms 02 / 03
Databricks

Azure Databricks Announces Lakebase GA, Lakeflow Free Tier at FabCon 2026

Databricks used FabCon 2026 to announce general availability of Lakebase, a managed serverless Postgres service built directly into the lakehouse and designed to serve as the transactional system of record for AI agents. Additional announcements included a free tier for Lakeflow Connect, new Microsoft 365 integrations, and AI-powered analytics through Genie. These releases position Azure Databricks as a full-stack solution from data ingestion through agent-ready transactional storage.

Why it matters Lakebase GA fills a critical gap by providing AI agents with a reliable, AI-native transactional data store inside the lakehouse, enabling more autonomous and reliable agentic workflows.
Data Platforms 03 / 03
Microsoft / Databricks

Microsoft and Databricks Deepen OneLake Integration with Unity Catalog Preview

Native reading from OneLake through Azure Databricks Unity Catalog has entered public preview, while Snowflake interoperability has reached general availability. Databricks will also add support for writing data directly into OneLake without additional storage resources, simplifying lakehouse architecture for joint customers. The deepening integration between the two platforms reflects an industry-wide push toward open, interoperable data ecosystems over proprietary silos.

Why it matters Seamless OneLake interoperability reduces data duplication costs and complexity for enterprises running both Fabric and Databricks workloads, a common scenario in large financial institutions.

Microsoft AI

Microsoft Foundry adds Claude models, RAG goes plug-and-play, and Azure HorizonDB arrives as a purpose-built AI database.

Microsoft AI 01 / 03
Microsoft

Azure AI Foundry Rebranded to Microsoft Foundry; Claude Models Now Available

Microsoft rebranded Azure AI Foundry to Microsoft Foundry in January 2026, signaling a broader platform vision beyond Azure-only deployments. Anthropic's Claude models have been added to Microsoft Foundry, joining OpenAI's GPT models to give enterprise developers a choice of frontier models within a single governed platform. Foundry IQ, a new capability, simplifies connecting disparate systems and data sources for building agentic AI applications.

Why it matters Adding Claude to Microsoft Foundry gives enterprises more model optionality within a trusted, compliant platform—reducing vendor lock-in concerns and enabling best-model-for-task strategies.
Microsoft AI 02 / 03
Microsoft Build

Microsoft Build 2026: RAG Goes Plug-and-Play, Local AI Models Now Supported

At Build 2026, Microsoft announced that Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is now plug-and-play on Azure, allowing developers to feed in enterprise content and receive grounded, lower-hallucination AI responses with minimal configuration. Azure also now supports smaller, efficient AI models that can run privately on local machines—a significant step for data-sensitive industries. Prompt Flow expanded support to include models from Meta and Mistral, with improved tracing and debugging capabilities.

Why it matters Plug-and-play RAG and local model support dramatically lower the barrier for financial institutions to deploy AI against proprietary, sensitive data without sending it to external endpoints.
Microsoft AI 03 / 03
Microsoft Azure

Azure HorizonDB Launches with Native Vector Indexing for AI Workloads

Microsoft introduced Azure HorizonDB, an AI-optimized database with built-in vector indexing, designed to support the retrieval and memory needs of agentic AI systems at enterprise scale. The database complements Microsoft Foundry's agentic infrastructure by providing purpose-built storage for embeddings and semantic search. Microsoft's stated goal is to make agentic AI a standard, not niche, capability for all Azure customers building and operating modern applications.

Why it matters A native AI-optimized database from Microsoft removes the need for third-party vector database solutions, simplifying architecture and reducing costs for enterprises building RAG and agent-based applications.
